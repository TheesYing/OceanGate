The Bodyguard

The Bodyguard: Original Soundtrack Album is the soundtrack album for the film of the same name, released on November 17, 1992 by Arista Records. The first side of the album (on the original LP) includes songs performed by Whitney Houston, who starred in the film, while the back side features works by various other artists. It is co-executively produced by Houston and Clive Davis.
The Bodyguard: Original Soundtrack Album received positive reviews from music critics. Commercially, the album reached the top of the album charts in all the countries it appeared in. In the United States, it topped the Billboard 200 for 20 non-consecutive weeks, and made Houston the first artist in the Nielsen SoundScan era (single or group, male or female) to have an album to sell. over a million copies in a week. The album has been certified 18 platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA), acknowledging that 18 million albums have been sold there.[2][3] To date, it has sold more than 55 million copies worldwide, making it the best-selling soundtrack album of all time, as well as one of the best-selling albums of all time.[4]
Seven singles were released from the album, the most successful of which was the lead single "I Will Always Love You", a cover of the original version by Dolly Parton. It has achieved tremendous success globally, becoming one of the most successful singles of the 1990s. The song became the best-selling single by a female artist with more than 22 million copies sold. released, and won two Grammy Awards for Record of the Year and Best Female Pop Vocal Performance. Her next two singles, "I'm Every Woman" and "I Have Nothing", both reached the top 5 on the Billboard Hot 100, making her the first female artist to have three songs in the top. 20 at the same time. In addition, "I Have Nothing" and "Run to You" were nominated for the Academy Award for Best Original Song in 1992.
The success of The Bodyguard soundtrack further cemented Houston's status as one of the most successful female artists. It was one of the best-selling albums of the 1990s globally, and broke sales records in many countries. In addition, it is the best-selling soundtrack album of all time in the United States as well as the sixth best-selling soundtrack of all time in the Soundscan era.[5] Following its resounding success, the singer has continued to reap numerous awards and nominations, including wins at the American Music Awards, Billboard Music Awards, Brit Awards, Juno Awards, and awards. Grammy in the Album of the Year category in 1994.
