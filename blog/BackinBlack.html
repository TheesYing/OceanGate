Back in Black

Back in Black is the seventh studio album by Australian rock band AC/DC, released on 25 July 1980 by Albert Productions and Atlantic Records. This is the band's first album to feature lead vocalist Brian Johnson since the death of former vocalist Bon Scott. After making a commercial breakthrough with the album Highway to Hell (1979), AC/DC was planning to record a sequel, but in February 1980, Scott died of alcohol poisoning from binge drinking. However, instead of being disbanded, the group decided to continue their activities and recruited Johnson - former vocalist of the band Geordie to replace Scott.
The album was co-produced by Johnson, Angus and Malcolm Young and was recorded over seven weeks in the Bahamas from April to May 1980 with producer Robert John "Mutt" Lange, who had worked with AC/DC in previous products. After completing the song, the band mixed for Back in Black at Electric Lady Studios in New York City.
SNot only is AC/DC's 6th album released on the international market, Back in Black has also achieved unprecedented success. As of 2011, Back in Black has sold approximately 50 million copies worldwide, making it the second best-selling album in history, behind only Michael Jackson's Thriller.[1][2][3][4] The band supported the album's promotion with a year-long world tour, placing them among the most popular artists of the early 1980s. The song received generally positive reviews. critically acclaimed in the early days of the album's release and has since been on numerous "best album" lists. Since the original release, the album has been republished and post-processed several times, most recently for digital distribution. On December 9, 2019, Back in Black received 25 platinum certifications by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).[5]
