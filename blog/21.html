21

21 is the second studio album by British singer-songwriter Adele, released on 24 January 2011[1] in Europe respectively by XL Recordings and on 22 February 2011 in North America. by Columbia Records. The album title is named after Adele's age when she produced the album. In addition to bringing in the folk and soul influences of Motown, which appeared on her debut album 19, 21, she also drew inspiration from the American country music and southern blues that she was exposed to during the An Evening with Adele tour in the North. America in the period 2008-09.
Adele began composing 21 in April 2009 while still in a relationship with her boyfriend, who became the inspiration for the song. Unsatisfied with having to play the tragic role again as her debut song, she planned to compose faster tempo songs. However, the recording process soon stopped due to lack of ideas. She continued to produce albums shortly after her relationship broke down, while also incorporating grief and sadness into the songs. Adele has collaborated with many songwriters and producers, including Columbia Records co-president Rick Rubin, Paul Epworth, Ryan Tedder, Jim Abbiss and Dan Wilson.
Thanks to the praise of Adele's simple production, distinctive aesthetics, and vocals from the industry, 21 defied the modest commercial predictions of XL Recordings to become a hugely successful album. of 2011. The album topped the charts in more than 30 countries and became the best-selling album of the year in both 2011 and 2012. In the UK, it is the best-selling album of the 21st century in the UK and the best-selling album of the year. fourth of all time; while 23 weeks at the top of the UK Albums Chart is also the record for the longest time at number one by a female solo artist. In the United States, the album stayed at number one for 24 consecutive weeks, longer than any album since 1985 and the longest by a female solo artist in Billboard 200 history.[2] In addition, 21 also holds the record for a female artist's album with the most weeks on the Billboard 200.[3] The song has been certified diamond by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) and is ranked as "The Greatest Album of All Time on the Billboard 200",[2] and holds more certifications than any other. No other album with more than 400 times certified around the world.
TFive singles were released to promote the album, with "Rolling in the Deep", "Someone Like You" and "Set Fire to the Rain" becoming international number-one singles, while "Rumour Has" It" is in the top 20 in many European and North American countries. 21 was the best-selling record of 2011 and 2012, helping to revive the declining sales of the US and UK music industry. With sales of over 44 million copies worldwide,[4] 21 is one of the best-selling albums of all time. Critics hailed the album as a departure from the current state of musical melodrama and explicit sexual themes, and attributed the album's success to its autobiographical yet universalist songs. [5] Besides being shortlisted for the 2011 Mercury Prize, 21 won the Grammy Award for Album of the Year 2012 and the Brit Award for British Album of the Year.
